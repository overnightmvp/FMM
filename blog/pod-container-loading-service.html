Why Container Loading Is Different From Truck Loading

A portable container has two key differences from a standard moving truck:

  1. No ramp â€” most containers sit on the ground or on a small platform, not at truck-bed height. Items must be carried in, not rolled with a ramp dolly.
  2. Weight distribution matters more â€” a truck's suspension handles uneven loads better. Containers sit flat, and an unbalanced load can cause structural damage or shifting during transport.

Loading a PODS Container: Pro Tips

1. Load the Heaviest Items to the Back (Wheel End)

In PODS containers, the "wheel end" is typically the stronger end. Load appliances, dressers, and heavy furniture there.

2. Fill Vertically, Section by Section

Same as a truck â€” fill floor to ceiling before moving to the next section. Items support each other and don't shift.

3. Wrap Everything

A PODS container travels by truck over sometimes rough roads. Items that aren't wrapped will bounce and scratch. We wrap every piece of furniture in moving blankets before loading.

4. Boxes First (Bottom Layer)

Dense, heavy boxes form a stable base layer. Lighter, fragile items go on top.

5. Secure with Straps

Use ratchet straps tied to the internal tie-down rings inside the container. We include straps on every job.
