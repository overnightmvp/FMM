## What Affects the Price

### 1. Home Size (Biggest Factor)
The number of bedrooms (or more accurately, the number of items to move) is the primary driver. A 3-bedroom with minimal furniture costs less than a 2-bedroom packed to the ceiling.

### 2. Hours Required
Most labor-only services price based on home size and include a set number of hours. If the job runs longer, overage is charged hourly (typically $75–$120/hour per mover, or $100–$200/hour for the crew).

### 3. Number of Movers
- 2 movers: Standard for 1–3 bedrooms
- 3 movers: Recommended for 4+ bedrooms or very heavy loads
- 4+ movers: Large homes or commercial moves

### 4. Add-On Services
| Service | Typical Cost |
|---|---|
| Stairs fee | $30–$75 per flight |
| Bed assembly/disassembly | $50–$100 per bed |
| Packing service | $75–$150 per room |
| Rental truck arrangement | $100–$200 |

### 5. Season and Timing
Florida has strong seasonal pricing patterns:
- **Peak (Dec–Apr):** Snowbird season, higher demand, book 2–4 weeks ahead
- **Moderate (May–Aug):** Summer moving season, standard prices
- **Off-peak (Sep–Nov):** Pre-season, sometimes negotiable rates

### 6. Urban vs. Suburban
Downtown Fort Myers and Naples (high-rise condos, elevator moves, parking restrictions) may require slightly more time than suburban single-family homes.

## How to Get the Best Price

1. **Book in advance** (1–2 weeks) — same-day premiums apply
2. **Choose a weekday** — Friday and Saturday cost more on demand
3. **Have everything ready** — boxes packed, furniture disassembled before arrival
4. **Bundle add-ons** — ordering packing service with your move is cheaper than scheduling separately
5. **Compare 2–3 quotes** — but prioritize licensed, reviewed providers over the lowest price

## What to Watch Out For

### Low-Ball Quotes That Inflate
Some companies quote a low hourly rate, then slow-walk the job to run up hours. Warning signs:
- Hourly pricing only (no flat rate option)
- No clear estimate of hours required
- No mention of what happens after included hours

### Uninsured Providers
Craigslist movers often quote $150–$250 for a 2-bedroom. What they don't mention: no license, no insurance, no recourse if your furniture is damaged or a worker is injured on your property.

### Hidden Fees
Always ask about: fuel surcharges, truck/equipment fees, minimum hours, cancellation policy.
